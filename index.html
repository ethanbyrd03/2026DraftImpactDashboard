01 / OVERVIEW
How the NBA impact score works
This model combines three analytical frameworks to project which 2026 draft prospects will have the highest impact at the NBA level. Raw college statistics alone are poor predictors of NBA success because they ignore two critical variables: how old a player is relative to their competition, and how good their competition actually was. These adjustments correct for both.
02 / RAW BPM
Box Plus/Minus
BPM estimates a player's impact per 100 possessions relative to a league-average player. It is calculated from box score statistics (points, rebounds, assists, steals, blocks, turnovers, usage) using a regression model calibrated to actual on-court +/− data.

OBPM measures offensive impact.
DBPM measures defensive impact.
BPM = OBPM + DBPM

Data sourced from Tankathon / BartTorvik for the 2025–26 college season (per-36 minute basis, updated through late March 2026).
03 / AGE ADJUSTMENT
Engelmann Age Correction
Younger players are statistically underrated by raw box scores. A 19-year-old dominating college basketball is a stronger signal than a 23-year-old doing the same — age relative to competition peers matters enormously.

Per the Engelmann / Royce Webb methodology:
Baseline age = 19.0 years
age_delta = 19.0 − player_age

adj_OBPM += age_delta × 0.55
adj_DBPM += age_delta × 0.35
A player 1 year younger than the baseline gains +0.90 total BPM. A 23-year-old is penalized −3.60 BPM.
→ Royce Webb / Engelmann methodology
04 / SOS ADJUSTMENT
Strength of Schedule
A player producing elite BPM against a weak mid-major schedule is less impressive than the same numbers against Power Five competition. We estimate each program's average opponent SRS and adjust toward a common baseline.

SOS baseline = 7.0 (avg SRS for historical draft classes)
sos_delta = (player_sos − 7.0) × 0.40

adj_OBPM += sos_delta
adj_DBPM unchanged (DBPM is SOS-invariant)
SOS estimates are derived from conference tier and team NET / KenPom data for the 2025–26 season.
05 / COMPOSITE SCORE
Impact score formula
The composite impact score weights adjusted metrics into a single ranking:
Impact =
  adj_BPM × 0.40
 + adj_OBPM × 0.25
 + adj_DBPM × 0.20
 + age_factor × 0.10
 + usage_factor × 0.05
age_factor — 0–10 scale. 19-yr-old = 5.0; each year younger adds +1.2.
usage_factor — 0–10 scale. 35% usage = 10.0.

This is a statistical model only. It does not account for medical history, position scarcity, character, or team fit.
06 / KEY FINDINGS
What the model shows
Cameron Boozer tops the model — the highest raw BPM (18.7) in the class combined with an 18.9-year-old age bonus on a tough Duke schedule.

Yaxel Lendeborg drops furthest: elite 16.7 raw BPM is heavily penalized at age 23.7, making his adjusted output far more modest.

Zuby Ejiofor is a notable model riser: raw BPM 14.5 at St. John's with a tough SOS earns a strong adjusted score despite a lower Tankathon rank.

International players (López, de Larrea, Daniels, Faye, Ruzic) are shown with available raw stats but estimated impact scores due to missing college BPM data.
